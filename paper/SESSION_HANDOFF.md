## 3. Headline numbers (best configuration per method, pooled, with \votag-PS/\votag-PA fallback for §5.5 only)

From [`paper/tables/method_comparison.tex`](tables/method_comparison.tex). Read the table for the full 4×3 grid; this is the summary.

- \bragtag\ (PS at best $k$) wins macro $F_1$ on Qwen-3B and Qwen-32B; fine-tune (PA) wins on Qwen-7B and Qwen-14B. Max gap between best two methods is **0.014** (Qwen-14B); max gap between \bragtag and fine-tune is 0.021 (Qwen-32B).
- \bragtag delivers the best question $F_1$ on three of four models; fine-tune wins question only on Qwen-14B. Bug and feature wins are split 2-2 between \bragtag and fine-tune.
- \bragtag is the most class-balanced: per-class $F_1$ std 0.053 (vs 0.066 fine-tune, 0.076 \ragtag); worst-class floor 0.694 (vs 0.672, 0.639).
- Cost crossover: fine-tune is more expensive at smaller sizes; **at Qwen-32B fine-tune is cheaper (2.71 h) than \ragtag/\bragtag (4.15–4.62 h)** because RAGTAG inference at $k$=12 processes ~20M prompt tokens, exceeding fine-tune's training+inference token volume.
