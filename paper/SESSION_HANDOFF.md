## 3. §5.5 headline framing (locked in)

The §5.5 narrative deliberately leads with \ragtag-as-competitive, then \bragtag-closes-gap. This positioning was a paper-level decision — see "Decisions logged" §6 below and the §6 discussion TODO entry. Numbers below are summary only; full per-model details in [`paper/tables/method_comparison.tex`](tables/method_comparison.tex).

- **\ragtag-PS stays close to fine-tune-PA in macro $F_1$.** Aggregate (\ragtag − fine-tune) difference: $-0.020$ with paired bootstrap 95\% CI $[-0.028, -0.013]$. Statistically tied at Qwen-32B; gap is largest at Qwen-14B ($-0.040$). Pattern: closer at the smallest and largest models, farther in the middle.
- **\bragtag's debiasing intervention closes the remaining gap to statistical equivalence.** Aggregate (\bragtag − fine-tune) difference: $+0.001$ with paired bootstrap 95\% CI $[-0.007, +0.008]$, **passes TOST equivalence at $\delta=0.01$**. Per-model deltas: $+0.009$ (3B), $-0.011$ (7B), $-0.014$ (14B), $+0.021$ (32B); none individually statistically significant.
- **Cost edge.** \ragtag/\bragtag use **33\% less peak GPU memory** than fine-tune averaged across the four sizes (47/31/25/29\% less at 3B/7B/14B/32B). \ragtag/\bragtag are faster than fine-tune at Qwen-3B/7B/14B; fine-tune is faster at Qwen-32B (cost analysis breakdown in [`paper/figures/cost_analysis.pdf`](figures/cost_analysis.pdf)).
- **Data scope edge.** \ragtag/\bragtag-PS retrieve from 300 labeled issues per project; fine-tune-PA needs 3,300. **11× less labeled data per project.**
- **Per-class.** \bragtag wins question $F_1$ on 3 of 4 models (loses only on Qwen-14B). Bug and feature wins are split 2-2 between fine-tune and \bragtag. \bragtag has the lowest per-class std (0.053) and highest worst-class floor (0.694).

## 6. Decisions logged

- **§5.5 narrative arc (paper-level decision).** §5.5 leads with "\ragtag stays close to fine-tune" then "\bragtag closes the remaining gap" — RAG-as-competitive is the defense layer, BRAG-closes-gap is the technical contribution. This protects the paper from a reviewer who guts the BRAG=FT equivalence claim, since RAG-as-competitive remains a contribution on its own. Same arc carries §6.
- **TOST equivalence margin: $\delta=0.01$.** Aggregate \bragtag-vs-fine-tune CI [-0.007, +0.008] fits inside ±0.01, so the tighter margin is defensible and is the one we report.
- **\votag-PS rescue is "free":** no LLM inference, reuses the same top-$k$ neighbors already retrieved for \ragtag/\bragtag few-shot prompts. \votag-PA used for fine-tune (no shared retrieval there, but rescue cost is still trivial — KNN voting only).
- **cost_analysis figure replaces method_pareto.** Two-panel figure (RAM bar + total-time stacked) tells the cost story cleanly. Pareto figure preserved on disk but no longer referenced in the paper.
- **DeBERTa-v3-large PA fine-tune: dropped.** Mode-collapsed (predicts only `bug`); macro $F_1$ = 0.167. Outputs preserved at `results/issues11k/agnostic/microsoft_deberta-v3-large/finetune_transformer/` for audit.
- **k=12/15 extension verdict.** Larger models (14B/32B) peak at $k$=12 for \ragtag and $k$=12–15 for \bragtag; smaller models (3B/7B) peak at $k$=3–6.
